Hello, my name is Jared Lewis, currently a junior at Arsenal Technical High School. Ever since I was younger, I've had a fascination with building things, whether it was constructing model cities with Lego bricks or assembling DIY projects with my dad in the garage. This hands-on experience sparked my interest in engineering and technology from an early age. Recently, I've developed a keen interest in coding, finding joy in the logic and creativity of programming languages. My diverse aspirations range from robotics and coding to soccer and representing my country in the World Cup. After much reflection, I've focused my ambitions on developing, building, and programming robots with the ultimate goal of making my mom proud through my achievements. I envision creating robots that profoundly impact people's lives, whether by designing fully autonomous public transportation buses to enhance daily commutes or developing robotic prosthetics to restore mobility for those in need. Beyond academics, I actively participate in robotics clubs, soccer teams, and have engaged in various camps and internships to broaden my skills. Looking forward, I plan to pursue mechanical engineering at Purdue University, undecided yet considering a double major or minor in computer science. In my spare time, I find joy in staying active with workouts and playing soccer.
